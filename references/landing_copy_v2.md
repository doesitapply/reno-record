## SECTION 2: THE ORIGIN — What the State's Own Documents Say

**Headline:** The Defense Theory Was in the Arrest Report

On March 10, 2023, a motorcycle was moved from a shared residence in Reno, Nevada. The registered owner, Brandon Pearson, filed a theft report. Cameron Church was arrested the following morning.

The probable-cause declaration — written by the arresting officer, filed by the State — records that Church told law enforcement the motorcycle was collateral for unpaid rent owed to him by Pearson. This is not a defense invented later. It is in the State's own paperwork, from the night of the arrest.

The Washoe County District Attorney charged the matter as Grand Larceny of a Motor Vehicle (NRS 205.228, Category C Felony) and Unlawful Taking of a Motor Vehicle (NRS 205.2715, Gross Misdemeanor).

The DA's own no-bail motion, filed April 18, 2025, describes the underlying facts this way: *"Cameron Church stole his roommate's motorcycle over a rent dispute."* The framing confirms the dispute's nature. The legal question — whether holding collateral for unpaid rent constitutes theft — was never adjudicated. There has been no trial.

> "Cameron Church stole his roommate's motorcycle over a rent dispute."
> — DA Aziz Merchant, Motion for No Bail Hold, April 18, 2025 (CR23-0657)

## SECTION 3: THE COUNSEL CAROUSEL — Five Attorneys, Zero Substantive Motions

**Headline:** Four Public Defenders and a Private Attorney. No Trial. No Motions.

Between March 2023 and the present, five attorneys have been assigned to or retained for this case. The docket does not reflect a single substantive motion filed on Church's behalf by any of them.

| Attorney | Role | Period | Exit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cooper Brinson | Alternate Public Defender | March 2023 – January 17, 2024 | Substituted out; left APD entirely shortly after |
| Sydney McBride Hutt | Alternate Public Defender | January 17, 2024 – May 30, 2024 | Declared conflict |
| Galen D. Carrico | Conflict counsel | June 24, 2024 – May 8, 2025 | Moved to withdraw |
| [Conflict group] | Unknown | May–June 2025 | Unknown |
| Samuel Figueroa | Private counsel | ~June 2025 – August 2025 | Sought withdrawal; told court Cameron "consents and wants to represent himself" |

**Brinson** reviewed the case early and characterized Church's factual defense as "fantastic," according to Church's account. He indicated he planned to pitch dismissal before the preliminary hearing. No motion to dismiss appears in the docket. On January 17, 2024, he was substituted out. He subsequently gave 14-day notice and resigned from the Alternate Public Defender's Office entirely.

**Hutt** declared a conflict on May 30, 2024, after approximately four months on the case.

**Carrico** filed a Motion for a Young Hearing on September 11, 2024 — a proceeding under NRS 178.388 to evaluate whether counsel is providing effective assistance. Two Young hearings were held: May 30, 2024 and December 5, 2024. Carrico moved to withdraw on May 8, 2025.

**Figueroa** was privately retained. His documented communications with Church consist of: a one-line email notifying Church of a settlement conference date, and a 10-minute warning email on the morning of the August 21, 2025 OSC hearing that resulted in a no-bail bench warrant. In a court email chain that included DA Merchant, Figueroa wrote: *"I want to withdraw, Mr. Church consents and wants to represent himself."*

Throughout this period, Church filed his own motions — raising Faretta rights, speedy trial violations, competency order challenges, and ineffective assistance claims. Every pro se filing was stamped with a notation: "DFX: DEFENDANT REPRESENTED BY COUNSEL." They were struck without ruling on the merits.

**Source callout:**
> "I want to withdraw, Mr. Church consents and wants to represent himself."
> — Samuel Figueroa, email to court and DA Merchant, August 12, 2025

## SECTION 4: THE DECEMBER 5 PIVOT — The Hearing That Changed Everything

**Headline:** A Judge Cited "Micro-Focus" and Constitutional Arguments as Grounds for a Competency Evaluation

On December 5, 2024, a Young hearing was held before Judge Barry L. Breslow, Department 8, Second Judicial District Court.

According to Church's filings — which include audio recordings of the hearing — the following occurred:

**1. Faretta rights denied without analysis.** Church made clear, unequivocal requests to represent himself. No Faretta hearing was conducted. No legal standard was applied. The request was not granted or formally denied — it was bypassed.

**2. Competency evaluation ordered without medical basis.** Judge Breslow ordered a psychiatric evaluation under NRS 178.400. The stated basis, per Church's account of the hearing, was Church's "micro-focus" on his case and the quality of his constitutional arguments. No prior psychiatric evaluation existed. No medical professional had raised competency concerns. NRS 178.400 requires "reasonable grounds" to believe a defendant may be incompetent — the standard is medical, not argumentative.

**3. Blanket prohibition on pro se filings.** The court issued an order prohibiting Church from filing anything on his own behalf while represented by counsel.

Two weeks later, on December 19, 2024, a no-bail bench warrant was issued. Church's address — 1605 McKinley Drive, Reno, NV 89509 — was on file with the court. The warrant was not served for four months.

Church's March 19, 2025 motion states: *"Defendant has never been found incompetent by any medical or psychological professional. There is no documented history of mental illness. There is no record of Defendant being unable to communicate or assist in his own defense. In fact, Defendant has consistently engaged in litigation, drafted substantive motions, and filed constitutionally sound challenges — all of which the Court has either ignored or actively suppressed."*

**Source callout:**
> "The Court has manufactured a procedural deadlock — removing Defendant's autonomy, delaying a trial without basis, and weaponizing competency as a stall tactic."
> — Church, Motion to Strike Competency Order, March 19, 2025 (CR23-0657)

## SECTION 5: THE OPERATION — US Marshals, a Tracker, and a Ruse

**Headline:** The DA's Own Motion Describes What It Took to Arrest Someone in a Rent Dispute

The bench warrant issued December 19, 2024 sat unserved for four months. Church's address was on file. He was filing motions with the court. He was in contact with his attorney.

On April 17, 2025, the warrant was executed. The DA's own motion for a no-bail hold, filed the following day, describes what happened:

> "law enforcement to include the federal US Marshall's Service, worked diligently to affect his capture with the use of a tracker on a vehicle associated with him and worked for weeks to pattern his movements. The first attempt to arrest him was unsuccessful, with the Defendant barricading himself in his house for days and refusing to come out. He was not captured willingly, but rather through a ruse that got him to eventually exit his home."

This is the State's own description, in a court filing, of the resources deployed to execute a bench warrant on a non-violent property dispute case:

- **US Marshals Service** (federal law enforcement)
- **Vehicle tracker** placed on a vehicle associated with Church
- **Weeks of surveillance** to "pattern his movements"
- **Multiple attempts**, including one where Church barricaded himself inside his home
- **A ruse** — a deceptive operation — to get him to exit

The DA's motion was filed to support a no-bail hold. It was granted. Church was held without bail pending competency proceedings and trial.

**Source callout:**
> "He was not captured willingly, but rather through a ruse that got him to eventually exit his home."
> — DA Aziz Merchant, Motion for No Bail Hold, April 18, 2025 (CR23-0657)

## SECTION 6: THE COMPARATOR — Richard Joyce and the Stabbing

**Headline:** The Man Who Stabbed Church Got His Charges Dismissed. Church's Bail Was $25,000 Cash-Only.

On April 10, 2023 — one month after Church's arrest — Richard Joyce stabbed Church. Joyce was charged in State v. Richard Joyce, RCR2023-122783. His bail was reduced from $150,000 to $500. His charges were dismissed at the preliminary hearing.

Church's bail on the motorcycle case: $25,000 cash-only. His pretrial release was revoked in October 2023 after a second arrest. His mother posted the $25,000 cash bond on October 17, 2023.

The prosecutor who handled Joyce's case — Chief Deputy District Attorney Amos Stege — is not assigned to CR23-0657. He does not appear in the case docket as counsel of record.

On September 26, 2025, Stege filed a "Notice of Violation of Bail Conditions" in Church's case. The exhibit attached to that notice was an email Church had sent to DA Merchant about a medical emergency — a suspected DVT blood clot — in which Church asked whether the State would attempt to execute the bench warrant if he sought emergency medical treatment.

Church's September 28, 2025 motion states: *"Stege... is disqualified by conflict of interest, having previously prosecuted State v. Richard Joyce (RCR2023-122783) where Defendant was the stabbing victim. That case was dismissed just before additional charges were stacked into the instant case."*

The court has not ruled on this conflict claim.

**Headline:** The Case Is Stayed. The Judge Is Being Reviewed. The Defendant Is Still Filing from the Same Address.

As of July 7, 2026 — the date of the most recent docket entry reviewed for this archive — the case status is:

- **June 16, 2026:** Order Staying Case issued by Judge Breslow, Department 8
- **June 22, 2026:** Order Referring Disqualification Question — a judicial disqualification or recusal question has been referred to another judge for determination
- **Active bench warrant:** NO BAIL HOLD, issued August 22, 2025
- **No trial date set**
- **Case has been pending for over 3 years** since arrest (March 2023)

Church continues to file pro se motions. His most recent filings include a "Master Structural Injury Motion for Specific Written Findings of Fact and Conclusions of Law, or Dismissal With Prejudice If the Court Cannot Explain Its Own Record" (May 20, 2026) and a motion demanding the court identify "the operative legal authority governing this case" and "terminate custody-conditioned adjudication" (May 14, 2026).

The court has not issued written findings in response to any of these motions.

**The 60-day rule** — Nevada's constitutional speedy trial guarantee — was waived at arraignment on September 12, 2023. Church has raised speedy trial violations in multiple filings. No ruling has been issued on those claims.

## SECTION 8: THE FEDERAL CASE

**Headline:** Church v. Breslow et al., 3:24-cv-00579-ART-CSD

A federal civil rights lawsuit is pending in the United States District Court for the District of Nevada. The case number is 3:24-cv-00579-ART-CSD. Named defendants include Judge Barry L. Breslow and other officials.

The claims, as described in Church's filings, include violations of the First, Sixth, and Fourteenth Amendments, and claims under 42 U.S.C. § 1983.

DA Merchant, who continues to prosecute CR23-0657, is named in the federal lawsuit. Church's September 2025 email to Merchant notes this directly: *"Despite naming both you personally and your employer in a pending federal civil rights lawsuit (3:24-cv-00579-ART-CSD), you've somehow managed to maintain your role as prosecutor in my criminal case."*

The court has not addressed this conflict on the record in CR23-0657.
